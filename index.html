01 — Background

Security from the attacker's
side of the keyboard

I'm a penetration tester based in Dubai. Right now I work at a school in Sharjah, where I run internal penetration tests and security engineering alongside my IT responsibilities. I hold OSCP+ and CRTP. In one assessment I went from a low-privileged user to Domain Admin in under six hours, chaining Kerberoasting, ACL abuse, and unconstrained delegation, all mapped out with BloodHound.

I also have a strong networking background. I hold a CCNA and spent time as a network support engineer at an ISP (VTEL) in Jordan, which shaped how I read attack paths across enterprise environments. Alongside testing, I build and harden infrastructure, from standing up Active Directory to SOC automation. I'm now looking to move into a client-facing penetration testing consultancy role. I publish what I build and learn on my blog.
